# Poker Terms Glossary

## Purpose

This glossary defines all terms used by the coaching system.
Agents should use these definitions when explaining concepts to users.
User-facing definitions are adapted by level (see `directives/adapt_to_user_level.md`).

---

## A

**Action** — A decision made by a player: fold, check, call, bet, or raise.

**Aggressor** — The player who made the last bet or raise on a given street.

**Air** — A hand with no real value and no draws. Pure bluffing hand.

**All-in** — Committing all remaining chips to the pot.

**Ante** — A mandatory forced bet placed by all players before the hand begins.

---

## B

**BB (Big Blind)** — The mandatory bet placed by the player two positions left of the dealer.

**Backdoor draw** — A draw that needs two more specific cards (e.g., backdoor flush draw needs both turn and river to be the same suit).

**Bet** — Placing chips into the pot when no bet has been made this street.

**Bluff** — Betting or raising with a hand that is unlikely to be the best at showdown, hoping to make opponents fold.

**Board** — The community cards dealt face up in the center of the table.

**Board texture** — The characteristics of the community cards: wet/dry, connected/scattered, paired/unpaired.

**BTN (Button)** — The dealer position. Acts last postflop. Most advantageous position.

---

## C

**Call** — Matching the current bet to stay in the hand.

**Check** — Passing action to the next player without betting (only available when no bet has been made).

**Check-raise** — Checking first, then raising after an opponent bets.

**CO (Cutoff)** — The position one seat right of the button. Second-best position.

**Community cards** — Cards dealt face up and shared by all players.

**Continuation bet (C-bet)** — A bet on the flop by the preflop aggressor.

**Cooler** — A hand where both players have very strong hands, and losing was essentially unavoidable.

---

## D

**Dead money** — Chips in the pot from players who have already folded.

**Donk bet** — A bet made by an out-of-position player into the preflop aggressor (often a mistake for beginners).

**Draw** — A hand that is incomplete but can become strong with additional community cards.

---

## E

**Equity** — Your percentage chance of winning the hand if all cards are dealt out.

**EV (Expected Value)** — The average amount won or lost per action over many repetitions.

**OESD (Open-Ended Straight Draw)** — Four consecutive cards that can make a straight on either end (8 outs).

---

## F

**Fish** — A weak or inexperienced player. Not a term used in coaching (use "recreational player" instead).

**Flop** — The first three community cards dealt face up.

**Fold** — Discarding your hand and surrendering your interest in the pot.

**Fold equity** — The EV gained from making an opponent fold.

**Fold frequency** — How often an opponent folds to a specific type of bet.

---

## G

**GTO (Game Theory Optimal)** — A mathematically balanced strategy that cannot be exploited in the long run.

**Gutshot** — A straight draw that requires one specific rank to complete (4 outs). Also called an inside straight draw.

---

## H

**Hand** — The cards dealt to a player plus the community cards.

**Hand history** — A record of all actions in a played hand.

**Hand range** — The set of all possible hands a player could hold given their actions.

**Heads-up** — A hand between exactly two players.

**Hero** — The player making the decision (the user).

**Hole cards** — The two private cards dealt face down to each player.

---

## I

**Implied odds** — The additional chips you expect to win on future streets if you hit your draw.

**In position (IP)** — Acting after your opponent on each postflop street.

---

## K

**Kicker** — An unpaired card that helps break ties between equal ranks (e.g., AK beats AQ — K is the better kicker).

---

## L

**Leak** — A systematic strategic error that loses chips over time.

---

## M

**Made hand** — A hand that is already complete and strong (pair, two pair, straight, flush, etc.).

**MDF (Minimum Defense Frequency)** — The minimum percentage of your range you must call to prevent an opponent from profitably bluffing every time.

**Merged range** — A betting range that contains mostly value hands and no bluffs.

---

## O

**Outs** — Cards remaining in the deck that will improve your hand.

**Out of position (OOP)** — Acting before your opponent on each postflop street.

**Overbet** — A bet larger than the current pot size.

**Overcards** — Cards in your hand that rank higher than any card on the board.

**Overpair** — A pocket pair higher than any card on the board (e.g., KK on a J-7-2 board).

---

## P

**Polarized range** — A betting range that contains either very strong hands or bluffs, with nothing in between.

**Position** — Where a player sits relative to the dealer button, determining their action order.

**Pot** — All chips committed by all players in the current hand.

**Pot odds** — The ratio of the call amount to the total pot (call + current pot), representing the minimum equity needed to break even.

**Preflop** — The betting round before any community cards are dealt.

---

## R

**Raise** — Increasing a bet that has already been made.

**Range** — All possible hands a player could have based on how they have played.

**River** — The fifth and final community card.

**Royal Flush** — A♠K♠Q♠J♠T♠ — the best possible hand.

---

## S

**SB (Small Blind)** — The mandatory half-bet placed by the player immediately left of the dealer.

**Semi-bluff** — Betting or raising with a drawing hand that can improve to the best hand.

**Set** — Three of a kind made with a pocket pair and one matching community card (strongest form of trips).

**Showdown** — When remaining players reveal their hands at the end of the hand to determine the winner.

**Slow-play** — Checking or calling with a very strong hand to disguise its strength and trap opponents.

**Stack** — A player's remaining chips.

**Straight Flush** — Five consecutive cards of the same suit.

---

## T

**Top pair** — Pairing the highest card on the board with a hole card.

**TPTK** — Top Pair, Top Kicker. The best possible one-pair hand on a given board.

**Turn** — The fourth community card.

---

## V

**Value bet** — A bet made with the intention of being called by worse hands.

**Villain** — The opponent (any player other than the hero).

---

## W

**Wet board** — A board with many possible flush or straight draws.

**Whale** — An extremely loose/passive player. Not used in coaching.
